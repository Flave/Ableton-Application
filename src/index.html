It was during my studies where I discovered my interest in education — especially my interest in computer education. During a course wonderfully titled “Smart Toys” I got in touch with the teachings of Seymour Papert and Jean Piaget and discovered the field of Explorable Explanations.

In “Programming Blocks” we tried to teach kids the most basic building blocks of a computer program, such as conditions and functions, simply by attaching hexagon shaped blocks together.

View Project

With “Huggaballs” we tried to help kids record their own stories and secrets and share them with their friends. We built cuddly, round monsters that remember the things you tell them when you hug them strongly enough. They then swap their stories with another monster when bumped into each other.
